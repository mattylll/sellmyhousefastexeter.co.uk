Cash Buyers vs Estate Agents in Exeter: Which Is Right for You?

By Sell My House Fast Exeter

Two Routes to Selling Your Exeter Property

When you decide to sell your home in Exeter, two main options present themselves. The traditional route involves instructing an estate agent who will market your property, conduct viewings, and negotiate with prospective buyers. The alternative is selling directly to a cash buyer who can purchase your property without the need for mortgage approval, viewings, or a property chain. Both routes have their merits and their drawbacks, and the right choice depends on your priorities, your timeline, and the specifics of your property and situation.

Exeter's property market has its own particular characteristics that influence this decision. The city's role as Devon's capital, the university's impact on housing demand, the varied housing stock from Roman-era city walls to modern Pinhoe estates, and the seasonal patterns driven by tourism and the academic year all shape how properties sell and how long the process takes. Understanding these local factors is essential to making an informed choice.

The Estate Agent Route in Exeter

How It Works

Selling through an Exeter estate agent follows a well-established process. You invite agents to value your property, select one (or more, in the case of multi-agency instruction), sign a contract, and your home goes on the market. The agent markets through Rightmove, Zoopla, their own website, and window displays. They conduct viewings, field enquiries, and negotiate offers on your behalf.

Exeter has a healthy selection of estate agents, from national chains like Wilkinson Grant and Fulfords to independent agencies with deep local knowledge. The quality of service varies, and choosing the right agent is an important decision that affects both the price achieved and the time taken.

Typical Timeline

In the current Exeter market, the average time from listing to completion is four to six months. Well-priced, well-presented properties in popular areas like St Leonard's or Heavitree may sell faster. Properties needing work, those in slower-moving areas like parts of Wonford, or those at the higher price points seen in Topsham can take significantly longer.

The timeline breaks down roughly as follows: two to four weeks to generate initial interest and viewings, two to six weeks to agree a sale, then eight to twelve weeks for the conveyancing process. At any point during this period, the sale can fall through, sending the seller back to the beginning.

Costs

Exeter estate agents typically charge between 1% and 1.75% of the sale price, plus VAT. On a property selling at the Exeter average of £310,000, that equates to £3,720 to £6,510 plus VAT in agent fees. Sole agency agreements are cheaper than multi-agency, but restrict your options. On top of agent fees, sellers pay for their own conveyancing solicitor (typically £1,000 to £1,500), an Energy Performance Certificate (around £80 to £120), and ongoing costs including mortgage payments, council tax, insurance, and maintenance during the marketing period.

Advantages

The primary advantage of the estate agent route is price. In a well-functioning market, a properly marketed property should achieve close to its full market value. For sellers who have time, a property in good condition, and no complications, the traditional route generally delivers the highest gross sale price.

Disadvantages

The disadvantages centre on uncertainty and time. There is no guarantee of achieving a sale, and roughly one in three agreed sales falls through before completion. The process requires the property to be presentable for viewings over an extended period. And the seller has limited control over the timeline, with the completion date largely determined by the chain and the various parties' mortgage lenders.

The Cash Buyer Route in Exeter

How It Works

Selling to a cash buyer in Exeter is a fundamentally different process. You contact the buying company, provide details about your property, and receive a valuation followed by a formal cash offer, usually within 24 to 48 hours. If you accept, solicitors are instructed immediately, and because there is no mortgage application and no chain, the legal process moves quickly. Completion can happen in as little as seven days.

Typical Timeline

Most cash sales in Exeter complete within two to three weeks of the seller accepting the offer. The speed depends primarily on the conveyancing process, which is simpler without a mortgage lender in the equation. Sellers who need more time can choose a later completion date; the flexibility works both ways.

Costs

A reputable cash buyer charges no fees to the seller. There are no agent commissions, no marketing costs, and basic legal fees are typically covered by the buyer. The cost comes in the form of a lower sale price: cash buyer offers usually fall between 75% and 85% of the full market value. On an Exeter property worth £310,000, that could mean an offer of £232,500 to £263,500.

Advantages

Speed and certainty are the primary advantages. You know the price, you choose the date, and the sale goes through. There are no viewings, no chain risks, and no dependence on another buyer's mortgage. For sellers dealing with time pressure, financial difficulty, property in poor condition, or complex situations like probate or tenanted properties, these advantages can be decisive.

Disadvantages

The lower sale price is the main disadvantage. If your priority is achieving the absolute maximum price and you have the time and a suitable property, the estate agent route will typically deliver a higher gross figure, though the net difference after costs and risks is often smaller than it first appears.

Running the Numbers: An Exeter Example

Consider a three-bedroom Victorian terrace in St Thomas, valued at £280,000 on the open market.

Estate Agent Route

  • Sale price (after possible negotiation): £270,000
  • Estate agent fee (1.5% + VAT): £4,860
  • Conveyancing: £1,200
  • EPC: £100
  • Mortgage payments during 5-month sale: £5,000
  • Council tax during marketing: £750
  • Net proceeds: approximately £258,090
  • Risk of fall-through: approximately 30%

Cash Buyer Route

  • Cash offer (80% of value): £224,000
  • Fees: £0 (legal costs covered)
  • Additional holding costs: minimal (2-3 weeks)
  • Net proceeds: approximately £224,000
  • Risk of fall-through: less than 3%

The difference in this example is around £34,000 in favour of the estate agent route. However, that figure assumes the traditional sale completes successfully. If it falls through and needs to start again, the costs increase and the timeline doubles. When the risk-adjusted figures are calculated, the gap narrows considerably, and for sellers who value their time or face pressing circumstances, the cash buyer route can represent better overall value.

Which Is Right for Your Exeter Property?

The estate agent route is likely better if you have a well-maintained property in a popular Exeter neighbourhood, no particular time pressure, tolerance for the uncertainties of the process, and achieving the maximum price is your primary goal.

The cash buyer route is likely better if you need to sell quickly due to personal or financial circumstances, your property needs work that would deter mortgage-dependent buyers, you are dealing with a probate property, tenanted property, or other complication, you want certainty and control over the completion date, or you have already experienced a failed sale through traditional channels.

Many Exeter homeowners find that getting offers from both routes provides the clearest picture. Comparing a cash buyer offer against an estate agent valuation, and then honestly assessing the costs, risks, and timeline of each, leads to the most informed decision. Whatever your situation, from a waterside house in Topsham to a terrace in St Thomas, understanding both options puts you in control.
