Selling an Inherited Property in Exeter: A Practical Guide

By Sell My House Fast Exeter

Inheriting a Property in Exeter

Inheriting a property in Exeter can be both a financial opportunity and a practical challenge. Devon's capital has a housing market where average prices sit around £310,000, meaning that an inherited home represents a significant asset. But realising that asset involves a process that can be complex, time-consuming, and emotionally draining, particularly when the executor lives outside the area or the property needs substantial work.

This guide covers the practical steps involved in selling an inherited property in Exeter, the challenges specific to the local market, and the options available to executors and beneficiaries who want to move the process forward efficiently.

The Probate Process

Before an inherited property can be sold, probate must normally be granted. Probate is the legal process that confirms the executor's authority to deal with the deceased person's estate. In England and Wales, the process involves applying to the Probate Registry with the original will (if there is one), a death certificate, and an Inheritance Tax form.

The time taken to obtain probate varies. Straightforward applications may be processed in eight to twelve weeks, while more complex estates can take considerably longer. During this period, the property must be maintained, insured, and secured, and the executor is responsible for these obligations.

It is worth noting that a property can be marketed and offers accepted before probate is granted, though the legal completion cannot take place until the grant is in hand. This allows executors to get the selling process underway while the probate application is being processed, reducing the overall timeline.

Valuing an Inherited Exeter Property

An accurate valuation is needed both for Inheritance Tax purposes and for determining the sale price. The valuation for tax purposes should reflect the property's market value at the date of death, not its value at the time of eventual sale.

In Exeter, the variation between neighbourhoods makes this valuation particularly important. A Victorian house in Heavitree may be worth significantly more or less than a superficially similar property in St Thomas or Wonford. Factors including condition, modernisation, garden size, parking, and school catchments all influence value, and an inherited property that has not been updated for decades needs careful assessment.

We recommend getting at least two independent valuations from local Exeter estate agents, supplemented by a check of recent sold prices on the Land Registry for comparable properties in the same street or area. For Inheritance Tax purposes, a RICS valuation may be advisable, particularly for higher-value properties in areas like Topsham or St Leonard's where HMRC scrutiny is more likely.

Common Challenges with Inherited Properties in Exeter

Property Condition

The most common challenge with inherited properties is condition. A home occupied by the same person for thirty or forty years will inevitably reflect the tastes and maintenance standards of an earlier era. Kitchens from the 1970s, avocado bathroom suites, single glazing, outdated electrics, and deferred roof repairs are all common findings.

In Exeter's Victorian and Edwardian neighbourhoods, these issues are compounded by the age of the buildings themselves. Damp, timber decay, and structural movement in properties of this vintage can generate survey findings that cause traditional buyers to reduce their offers or withdraw entirely.

Distance Management

Many executors do not live in Exeter. Managing a property from London, the Midlands, or elsewhere creates practical difficulties. Arranging viewings, meeting tradespeople, monitoring the property's security, and dealing with the postal and administrative requirements of a sale all become more difficult and expensive when the executor cannot easily visit.

Multiple Beneficiaries

Where an estate is divided between several beneficiaries, all must agree on the selling strategy. Disagreements about price, timing, or whether to renovate before selling can create delays that extend the process significantly. A cash sale at a known price can resolve these disagreements by providing a definitive figure and timeline that all parties can evaluate.

Clearing the Property

A lifetime's accumulation of belongings must be sorted, removed, or disposed of before a property can be sold with vacant possession. This task, which can be physically and emotionally demanding, is another reason executors seek a swift sale. Some cash buyers, ourselves included, can work with properties that have not been fully cleared, reducing this burden.

Selling Options for Inherited Exeter Properties

Traditional Estate Agent

Marketing the property through an Exeter estate agent is an option if the property is in reasonable condition, the executor has time, and achieving the maximum price is the priority. Expect a timeline of four to six months or more, with agent fees of 1% to 2% plus VAT, legal costs, and the ongoing expenses of maintaining an empty property during marketing.

Auction

Property auctions can work for inherited properties, particularly those in poor condition that are likely to attract developer interest. The timeline is fixed (properties sell on auction day), and the sale is legally binding once the hammer falls. However, auction guide prices can be unpredictable, fees are higher than standard sales, and the format may not achieve the best price for properties in reasonable condition.

Cash Buyer

Selling to a cash buyer like us combines speed, certainty, and simplicity. We buy inherited properties in any condition, handle our own due diligence, and can complete as soon as probate is granted, or even exchange contracts conditionally before the grant is issued. There are no fees, we cover basic legal costs, and the executor retains full control over the completion date.

For executors managing Exeter properties from a distance, dealing with multiple beneficiaries, or facing a property that needs work they are unwilling or unable to fund, the cash buyer route often represents the most practical option.

Tax Considerations

Inheritance Tax applies to estates valued above the nil-rate band (currently £325,000, with an additional residence nil-rate band of £175,000 for direct descendants inheriting a home). Given Exeter's average prices, many inherited properties will push the total estate above these thresholds.

Capital Gains Tax may also apply if the property is sold for more than its probate value. The gain is calculated as the difference between the probate valuation and the sale price, less allowable costs. Getting the probate valuation right is therefore doubly important: too low, and you may face a larger CGT bill when you sell; too high, and you may overpay Inheritance Tax.

Professional tax advice is strongly recommended for any inherited property sale. The interaction between IHT and CGT, the available reliefs and exemptions, and the specific circumstances of the estate all affect the tax position.

Moving Forward

Selling an inherited property in Exeter does not have to be overwhelming. By understanding the probate process, getting accurate valuations, and choosing the selling route that matches your priorities and circumstances, you can resolve the estate efficiently and fairly. Whether you choose the traditional market, auction, or a cash buyer, the key is making an informed decision based on the specific situation of the property and the beneficiaries involved.
